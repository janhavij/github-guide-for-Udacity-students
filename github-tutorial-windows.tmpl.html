Setting up GitHub on Your Windows PC

An Easy Guide to Getting Your Project on GitHub

Click here to visit the GitHub for Windows website.
Click the "Download GitHub for Windows" button.
Open the file you just downloaded. Your computer may open a Security Warning window, that looks like following.
Click on "Install".
You will see the following window: "Installing GitHub", that shows the progress of installation.
Once the installation is complete, you will see the following screen, don’t worry if you don’t understand what "Extracting Git" means.
Finally, you should see the following window, that means your GitHub client is ready and you are only a couple of steps away from creating your first repository.
On your computer, navigate to the folder with your project and “drag and drop” it on the GitHub app.
Your GitHub app will look like following.
Click on "Create Repository". You should now see the files in your folder on the right side as shown below.
Under "Uncommitted Changes" you will see textbox for summary and description. Enter "First Commit" in the summary and "My first project for Nanodegree" in the description. This will make "Commit to master" clickable. It will also show you how many files you have in your repository. Click on "Commit to master" (Yes, this seems a bit strange, but you will have an opportunity to learn what these actions mean.)
Click on "Publish Repository". Login with your username and password, or if you don't have one head to GitHub.com to sign up.
You will now see the following screen.
Click on “Publish”.
Congratulations! You are ready to submit your project now. We look forward to your success! If you have any problems, please don't hesitate to email us at review-support@udacity.com.
